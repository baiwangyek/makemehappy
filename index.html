MAKE ME HAPPY

The science behind the why and how cute animals make us more happy

TLDR â€“ We are visual creatures and we are programmed to be susceptible to attributes associated with cuteness to trigger care-taking behaviours.

We have always been fascinated with cute animals. Heck, the internet, with its vast sea of knowledge, has always been associated with one thing - cute cats. Companies have also picked up on this phenomena and used cute puppies to sell toilet paper, and it worked!!

Clearly based on these evidences alone, it is obvious that cute animals are able to provoke a sense of positivity in all of us. But why?

Konrad Lorenz, a Nobel-winning Austrian zoologist was the first person to study this and is often thought of as the father of cuteness research. He observed a set of characteristics (large eyes, bulbous foreheads and small chins), in human and animal babies, that he termed "kindchenschema" and believed triggered care-taking behavior in a parent.

In one 2009 study, researchers manipulated pictures of babies to give them less pronounced baby features or more pronounced baby features. The participants felt a stronger drive to take care of babies with more more pronounced baby features.

In another study, researchers from Hiroshima University found people who had looked at pictures of baby animals subsequently did better in tasks that required concentration. It is suggested that we are more productive and attentive when we see cute animals.
